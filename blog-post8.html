Google Business Profile Categories: The Ultimate Guide

Choosing the right Google Business Profile (GBP) categories is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your local search visibility. Get it wrong, and you could be invisible to potential customers. Get it right, and you'll appear at the top of search results for the services you offer.

At Ranking Matters, we've helped hundreds of businesses optimize their GBP categories to achieve #1 rankings on Google Maps. In this ultimate guide, we'll show you exactly how to choose the right categories for your business.

Key Insight: Your GBP categories tell Google what your business is and who should see it. Choosing the right categories is critical for appearing in relevant local searches.

What Are Google Business Profile Categories?

Google Business Profile categories are labels that describe your business and the services you offer. They help Google understand what your business does so it can show your profile to people searching for relevant products or services.

There are two types of categories:

  • Primary Category: The main category that best describes your overall business. This is the most important category you'll choose.
  • Secondary Categories: Additional categories that describe other services or products you offer. You can add up to 9 additional categories.

Why Are GBP Categories So Important?

Your GBP categories directly impact your local search rankings. Here's why they matter:

  • Relevance: Categories signal to Google what your business is about. The more relevant your categories, the more likely you'll appear in relevant searches.
  • Visibility: The right categories help you appear in the Google Maps pack and local search results.
  • Traffic: Better visibility leads to more clicks, calls, and customers.
  • Competitive Advantage: Choosing specific, niche categories can help you stand out from competitors who choose generic categories.

Pro Tip: According to Google, businesses with relevant categories receive more than 40% more calls than businesses with irrelevant or missing categories.

How to Choose the Right Primary Category

Your primary category is the most important decision you'll make for your GBP. Here's how to choose it:

  1. Be Specific: Choose the most specific category that accurately describes your business. For example, instead of "Plumber," choose "Emergency Plumber" if that's your specialty.
  2. Focus on Your Core Service: Your primary category should reflect your main service offering. If you're a roofing company, choose "Roofing Contractor" as your primary category.
  3. Think Like a Customer: Consider what your customers would search for to find your business. Use those terms as your primary category.
  4. Avoid Keyword Stuffing: Don't try to use your primary category to stuff keywords. Google can detect this and may suspend your profile.

How to Choose Secondary Categories

Once you've chosen your primary category, you can add up to 9 secondary categories. Here's how to choose them:

  • Add Related Services: If you offer multiple services, add categories for each one. For example, a restaurant might add "Takeout Restaurant" and "Catering."
  • Think About Customer Intent: What are customers looking for when they search for your business? Choose categories that match their search intent.
  • Be Relevant: Only add categories that are truly relevant to your business. Adding irrelevant categories can hurt your rankings.
  • Update Regularly: If you add new services or products, update your secondary categories to reflect them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are some common mistakes businesses make when choosing GBP categories:

  • Using Generic Categories: "Professional Services" is too vague. Be specific about what you do.
  • Adding Irrelevant Categories: Don't add categories for services you don't offer just to attract more traffic.
  • Not Using All 10 Categories: You have 10 category slots available. Use them all with relevant categories.
  • Keyword Stuffing: Don't try to manipulate rankings by stuffing keywords into your category names.
  • Never Updating: If your business evolves, your categories should evolve too.

Warning: Adding irrelevant categories or keyword stuffing can lead to GMB suspension. Always be honest and accurate with your categories.

Industry-Specific Category Examples

Here are some examples of good category choices for different industries:

HVAC & Plumbing

  • Primary: HVAC Contractor
  • Secondary: Air Conditioning Contractor, Heating Contractor, Plumbing Contractor, Emergency Plumber

Restaurants & Cafes

  • Primary: Restaurant
  • Secondary: Italian Restaurant, Takeout Restaurant, Catering, Family Restaurant

Real Estate

  • Primary: Real Estate Agency
  • Secondary: Property Management, Real Estate Consultant, Home Staging
